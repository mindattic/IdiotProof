Volume Analysis

Volume is the most important confirmation indicator. Price movements on high volume are more significant than those on low volume.

Why Volume Matters

Volume shows conviction behind price moves. High volume on a breakout confirms institutional interest. Low volume suggests lack of commitment and potential for reversal.

💡 Key Insight

"Volume precedes price" - Volume often increases before major price moves, making it a leading indicator for breakouts and breakdowns.

Volume IdiotScript Commands

IsVolumeAbove(multiplier)

Returns true when current volume is above the specified multiple of average volume. Default period is 20 bars. 

Ticker(AAPL)
    .IsVolumeAbove(1.5)    // Volume >= 1.5x average
    .IsAboveVwap()
    .Entry(150)
    .TakeProfit(158)

IsHigherLows()

Returns true when the price pattern shows higher lows forming, indicating building bullish pressure. 

Ticker(NVDA)
    .IsHigherLows()       // Building support pattern
    .IsAboveVwap()
    .IsVolumeAbove(1.2)
    .Entry(900)
    .TakeProfit(920)

On-Balance Volume (OBV)

OBV is a cumulative indicator that adds volume on up days and subtracts volume on down days. It shows whether volume is flowing in or out of a stock.

OBV CALCULATION ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ If Close > Previous Close: OBV = Previous OBV + Current Volume If Close < Previous Close: OBV = Previous OBV - Current Volume If Close = Previous Close: OBV = Previous OBV (no change) OBV DIVERGENCE ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ BULLISH DIVERGENCE: Price down, OBV up Price │ │ \ │ \____ │ \____ ← Price making lower lows │ \ └─────────────────────────────────────────── OBV │ ____/ │ ____/ ← OBV making higher lows │ ____/ │ __/ (Accumulation happening) └─────────────────────────────────────────── BEARISH DIVERGENCE: Price up, OBV down Price │ ____/ │ ____/ ← Price making higher highs │ ____/ │ __/ └─────────────────────────────────────────── OBV │ │ \ │ \____ │ \____ ← OBV making lower highs │ \ (Distribution happening) └───────────────────────────────────────────

Volume in Market Score

Volume contributes 8% to the total market score. It acts as a confirmation/multiplier for other signals.

VOLUME SCORE CONTRIBUTION ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ Volume Ratio Direction Confirmation Score Contribution ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── 2.0x+ Price rising +100 2.0x+ Price falling -100 1.5x Price rising +75 1.5x Price falling -75 1.0x (average) Any direction 0 0.5x Against trend ±50 (dampened) Logic: Volume confirms the current price direction High volume + up move = bullish High volume + down move = bearish Low volume = less conviction in any direction
Condition Score Interpretation
High volume + bullish price +75 to +100 Strong buying conviction
Average volume 0 Normal activity
Low volume + bullish price +25 Weak rally, be cautious
High volume + bearish price -75 to -100 Strong selling pressure

Volume Warm-Up Requirements

Indicator Bars Needed Start Early By
Relative Volume 20 bars 25 minutes
OBV 1 bar (cumulative) 5 minutes
IsHigherLows 3+ bars 5 minutes
ℹ️ Note

Relative Volume needs 20 bars to establish a reliable average. IsVolumeAbove() signals are most reliable after 25+ minutes.

Volume Trading Strategies

1. Volume Confirmation Breakout

Only trade breakouts with volume confirmation.

Ticker(AAPL)
    .Name("Volume Breakout")
    .Session(IS.RTH)
    .Quantity(50)
    .IsVolumeAbove(1.5)    // Volume 1.5x or higher
    .IsAboveVwap()          // Price above VWAP
    .IsEmaAbove(9)          // Short-term bullish
    .IsMomentumAbove(0)     // Positive momentum
    .Entry(150)
    .TakeProfit(158)
    .StopLoss(147)
    .AdaptiveOrder(IS.AGGRESSIVE)

2. Volume + Higher Lows

Enter when building support with volume confirmation.

Ticker(NVDA)
    .Name("Higher Lows Volume")
    .Session(IS.RTH)
    .Quantity(20)
    .IsHigherLows()         // Building support
    .IsVolumeAbove(1.2)     // Above average volume
    .IsAboveVwap()          // Above fair value
    .Entry(900)
    .TakeProfit(930)
    .StopLoss(885)

3. High Volume Reversal

Look for reversals on volume spikes.

Ticker(TSLA)
    .Name("Volume Reversal")
    .Session(IS.RTH)
    .Quantity(15)
    .IsVolumeAbove(2.0)     // Significant volume spike
    .IsRsiOversold(30)      // Coming from oversold
    .IsEmaTurningUp(9)      // EMA turning
    .Entry(250)
    .TakeProfit(270)
    .StopLoss(240)

4. Low Volume Short (Weak Rally)

Short when rallies lack volume conviction.

Ticker(SPY)
    .Name("Weak Rally Short")
    .Session(IS.RTH)
    .Quantity(100)
    // Note: Would need IsVolumeBelow command
    .IsEmaBelow(21)         // Below trend EMA
    .IsBelowVwap()          // Below VWAP
    .IsDiNegative()         // Bearish direction
    .Order(IS.SHORT)
    .TakeProfit(495)
    .StopLoss(508)

Volume Trading Tips

💡 Pro Tips
  • Never trust a breakout without volume - Volume is the "truth serum" of price moves
  • Watch for volume at key levels - High volume at support/resistance is significant
  • Volume spikes often mark reversals - Climactic volume = potential exhaustion
  • Declining volume during rallies = weakness - Smart money may be distributing
  • Compare to recent average, not absolute levels - 1M volume means nothing without context
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Trading breakouts on low volume (false breakout risk)
  • Ignoring volume context in pre/post market (always lower)
  • Not waiting for volume average to establish (20-bar minimum)
  • Expecting volume patterns to work the same in all market conditions
