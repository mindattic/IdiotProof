Simulation & Risk Analysis

Monte Carlo simulation, paper trading, and risk analysis tools to understand strategy robustness before deploying real capital.

Monte Carlo Simulation

Monte Carlo simulation generates thousands of possible equity curves by randomizing trade order and/or sampling from historical trade outcomes. This reveals the range of possible outcomes and helps set realistic expectations.

MONTE CARLO CONCEPT ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ HISTORICAL TRADES: W, W, L, W, L, L, W, W, W, L (10 trades, 60% WR) Single Backtest (Specific Order): ┌──────────────────────────────────────┐ │ W→W→L→W→L→L→W→W→W→L │ P&L: +$620 └──────────────────────────────────────┘ Monte Carlo (1000 Random Orderings): ┌──────────────────────────────────────┐ │ Sim 1: L→W→W→L→W→W→L→W→W→L │ P&L: +$580 │ Sim 2: W→L→L→W→W→W→L→W→W→L │ P&L: +$650 │ Sim 3: L→L→L→W→W→W→W→W→W→L │ P&L: +$610 │ Sim 4: W→W→W→W→W→W→L→L→L→L │ P&L: +$540 │ ... │ │ Sim 1000: L→W→L→W→W→L→W→W→L→W │ P&L: +$625 └──────────────────────────────────────┘ Result: Distribution of Possible Outcomes P&L Distribution: ██ ██████ ██████████ ██████████████ ████████████████████ $400 $500 $600 $700 $800 $900 $1000 ↑ ↑ 5th %ile 95th %ile (worst) (best)

Running Monte Carlo

var simulator = new MonteCarloSimulator(backtestResults);

var monteCarlo = simulator.Run(
    iterations: 10000,
    sampleSize: 100  // trades per simulation
);

Console.WriteLine($"Median P&L: ${monteCarlo.MedianPnL:N0}");
Console.WriteLine($"5th %ile:   ${monteCarlo.Percentile5:N0}");
Console.WriteLine($"95th %ile:  ${monteCarlo.Percentile95:N0}");
Console.WriteLine($"Ruin Risk:  {monteCarlo.RuinProbability:P1}");

ℹ️ Interpreting Results

The 5th percentile represents a "bad luck" scenario - only 5% of simulations performed worse. Use this for position sizing and to set realistic minimum expectations.

Risk of Ruin

Risk of Ruin calculates the probability of losing a specified percentage of your account (e.g., 50%) before reaching a profit target.

RISK OF RUIN ANALYSIS ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ Account: $10,000 | Win Rate: 60% | Win/Loss Ratio: 1.5:1 Ruin Threshold Probability Risk Level ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── 25% loss ($7,500) 8.5% LOW 50% loss ($5,000) 1.2% VERY LOW 75% loss ($2,500) 0.1% MINIMAL ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── RUIN PROBABILITY BY RISK PER TRADE: Risk/Trade Ruin Prob (50%) Assessment ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── 1% 0.01% SAFE - Very conservative 2% 0.5% SAFE - Recommended 5% 12% MODERATE - Aggressive 10% 45% HIGH - Dangerous 20% 78% EXTREME - Gambling 
var ruin = simulator.CalculateRiskOfRuin(
    accountSize: 10000,
    ruinThreshold: 0.50,  // 50% loss
    riskPerTrade: 0.02    // 2% per trade
);

Console.WriteLine($"Probability of 50% loss: {ruin:P2}");

Paper Trading

Paper trading runs your strategy in real-time with simulated orders - no real money at risk. This is the bridge between backtesting and live trading.

PAPER TRADING MODE ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ LIVE MARKET DATA │ │ ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ │ │ Real prices, real-time updates │ │ │ │ From: Interactive Brokers TWS │ │ │ └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘ │ └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘ │ ▼ ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ STRATEGY ENGINE │ │ ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ │ │ Evaluates conditions │ │ │ │ Generates signals: BUY, SELL, HOLD │ │ │ └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘ │ └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘ │ ▼ ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ PAPER ORDER EXECUTOR (Simulated) │ │ ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ │ │ - No real orders sent │ │ │ │ - Simulated fills at current price + slippage │ │ │ │ - Virtual P&L tracking │ │ │ │ - All trades logged for analysis │ │ │ └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘ │ └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Enabling Paper Mode

// In configuration or at runtime:
tradingSettings.PaperTradingMode = true;

// Or via IdiotScript:
Ticker(NVDA)
.Session(IS.RTH)
.PaperTrade()  // Enable paper trading for this strategy
.AutonomousTrading()
⚠️ Paper Trading Limitations
  • No slippage reality - Paper fills at exact price, real fills may differ
  • No market impact - Your orders don't move the market in paper mode
  • No emotional pressure - Real money creates different psychology
  • No liquidity issues - Paper always fills, real markets may not

Best Practices

💡 Pro Tips
  • Run at least 1,000 iterations for reliable Monte Carlo results
  • Paper trade for 30+ days before going live
  • Size for the 95th percentile drawdown, not the median
  • Compare strategy to buy-and-hold as a baseline
  • Re-run simulations quarterly as more data accumulates
⚠️ Reality Check

Simulation results are based on historical data. Future market conditions may differ significantly. Use simulations to understand relative performance and risk characteristics, not to predict exact future returns.
